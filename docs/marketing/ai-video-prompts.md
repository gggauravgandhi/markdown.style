# AI video prompts

Prompts for text-to-video tools (Sora, Veo, Runway, Kling, Pixverse). Read this first:

**These are brand b-roll, not product demos.** AI video cannot render the real markdown.style UI, and it
renders readable text badly (garbled letters, fake words). So do not ask it to show the actual app or real
markdown. Use these atmospheric clips to open or bridge a video, then cut to a real screen recording for the
part that shows the product working. The screen recording is the proof. The AI clip is the mood.

Every prompt below already says no music and no audio, per the ask. Keep it that way: the brand is quiet.

Shared style to keep across all clips, so they feel like one set:
- Calm, precise, typographic. Think a quiet print studio, not a tech ad.
- Palette: paper white, near-black ink, one deep indigo accent, thin hairline rules. No neon, no gradient glow.
- Slow, steady camera. No whip pans, no fast zooms, no lens flare.
- Vertical 9:16 for reels. Ask for 16:9 only if you want a website hero loop.

---

## 1. Ink becoming a page (the core metaphor)

> A single sheet of clean white paper on a dark desk, shot from directly above, soft even studio light. Loose
> black text settles onto the page and arranges itself into neat aligned paragraphs and a clean ruled table.
> The motion is calm and deliberate, like typesetting. One thin indigo line draws itself under a heading.
> Minimal, elegant, precise. No music, no audio. No readable words, treat the text as abstract shapes. Slow
> static overhead shot, 9:16.

Use as: the opening three seconds before a screen recording.

---

## 2. One shape, many styles

> An abstract document made of soft grey blocks and lines floating on a pale background, seen straight on. The
> same block layout smoothly restyles itself several times: the fonts, spacing, and one accent color shift
> with each change while the overall shape stays the same. Calm, minimal, one change every second or so. Deep
> indigo, warm sand, and cool slate accents in turn. No music, no audio, no legible text. Gentle static
> camera, 9:16.

Use as: the "a style for every document" idea, without saying it.

---

## 3. The quiet print studio

> A tidy minimalist desk in soft morning light: a laptop turned slightly away so the screen is not readable, a
> stack of crisp printed documents, a single pen, a cup of coffee. A hand calmly squares the stack of pages
> and sets it down. Shallow depth of field, warm and unhurried, muted colors. No music, no audio. Slow push in,
> 9:16.

Use as: a human, grounded bridge shot. Pairs well with the "how I use it daily" angle.

---

## 4. Plain to polished, split down the middle

> A vertical split screen. On the left, a rough grey block of monospaced-looking text, plain and unstyled. On
> the right, the same block resolves into a clean, well set page with clear headings and a tidy table. The
> right side settles into place a beat after the left. Flat even lighting, paper white and ink black, one thin
> indigo rule between the halves. No music, no audio, no readable words. Static locked-off shot, 9:16.

Use as: the before and after, as pure shape. Cut to a real screen recording right after for the true version.

---

## 5. Paper and type, macro

> Extreme close-up of a freshly printed page, camera slowly gliding across crisp black serif text and a thin
> indigo underline on a heading. Fine paper texture, soft directional light, shallow focus so only one line is
> sharp at a time. Quiet, tactile, premium. No music, no audio. Treat the text as texture, not something to
> read. Slow lateral drift, 9:16.

Use as: a texture cutaway between two other clips.

---

## 6. A document assembling itself

> On a plain light surface seen from above, the parts of a document arrive one at a time and lock into place:
> first a title bar, then two columns of body lines, then a small ruled table, then a short block that looks
> like code. Each piece slides in cleanly and settles. Calm, architectural, no rush. Paper white with ink and a
> single indigo accent. No music, no audio, no legible text. Static overhead, 9:16.

Use as: a "structure, handled" feeling for the technical audience.

---

## 7. The stack of finished documents

> A slow reveal of several finished printed documents fanned out on a clean desk, each with a clearly different
> visual style: one formal and corporate, one academic and restrained, one bold and modern. Overhead soft
> light, calm and confident. The camera drifts just enough to show they are different from one another. No
> music, no audio, keep any text unreadable and abstract. Slow overhead drift, 9:16.

Use as: the closing shot before the end card, showing range.

---

## How to use these in a real post

1. Open with one AI clip (1, 3, or 4 work best as openers).
2. Cut to a real screen recording of markdown.style doing the actual thing. This is non-negotiable: the AI
   clip sets mood, the screen recording is the honest proof.
3. Close on clip 7 or a plain end card: `markdown.style`, `free, no signup`.

Keep the whole thing under twenty seconds. No music, per the brand and the ask. Captions, if any, in plain
sentence case, and never let an AI clip try to display a real claim or a real URL, since it will spell it
wrong.
