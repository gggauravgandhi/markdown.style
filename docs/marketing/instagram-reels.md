# Instagram reel scripts

Five short reels, mostly screen recordings. The tool is visual, so the screen does the talking and the
captions stay out of the way. Keep it calm. No hype words, no exclamation spam, no "you won't believe."
Let the transformation be the whole point.

## Direction notes for the whole set

- Record the screen at real speed, or speed up dead air only. Do not fake-speed the render, it is already fast.
- Captions in plain sentence case. Short. One idea on screen at a time.
- Music: low and steady, no drop, no build. If in doubt, no music and just the click sounds.
- Every reel ends on the same quiet end card: `markdown.style` and `free, no signup`.
- Shoot vertical, 1080x1920. Leave the top and bottom sixth clear of text so the Instagram UI does not cover it.
- Claims to keep honest (do not change these in a caption):
  - PDF is Save as PDF from the browser print box. Do not say "one-click PDF" or "instant PDF."
  - It styles and exports. It is not a full writing app.
  - Nothing is uploaded. Do not say "never touches the internet," since remote images in your markdown load normally.

---

## Reel 1: The transformation (silent, ~15s)

The core one. No voiceover. Let people watch plain text become a document.

**Scenes:**
1. (0 to 3s) A wall of raw markdown pasted in the box. Caption: `This is what AI gives you.`
2. (3 to 8s) Click a style. The document renders on the right. Caption: `This is what you can send.`
3. (8 to 12s) Click Export, the PDF saves. No caption, just the file landing.
4. (12 to 15s) End card.

**Post caption:**
Paste the markdown, pick a style, export. Your AI wrote the words. It does not have to typeset the page too.
Free, in the browser, at markdown.style.

---

## Reel 2: One doc, many styles (silent, ~12s)

Shows range without saying "30 themes." Let the eye count.

**Scenes:**
1. (0 to 2s) One rendered document on screen. Caption: `Same document.`
2. (2 to 11s) Cycle through six or seven styles, about one per second. Business, technical, academic, bold.
   Caption after the second switch: `Different style. Same words.`
3. (11 to 12s) End card.

**Post caption:**
A report should not look like a research paper. A spec should not look like a memo. Pick the one that fits.

---

## Reel 3: The reformatting loop (talking head or text, ~20s)

The pain, told flat. If your friend is on camera, keep the delivery dry, almost bored. No selling.

**Voiceover or on-screen text, one line per beat:**
1. You ask AI for a doc. It is good.
2. You ask it to make a PDF. It gives you a mess.
3. You ask it to fix the design. It rewrites your content.
4. You fix a typo. The styling resets.
5. (cut to screen record of markdown.style) Or you paste it here once, and style it as many times as you want.

**Post caption:**
Generate the content once. Handle the look locally, for free, without another prompt.

---

## Reel 4: It handles the messy stuff (silent, ~15s)

Proof for the skeptics. Show the things that usually break.

**Scenes:**
1. (0 to 4s) Scroll a rendered doc past a table, then syntax-highlighted code. Caption: `Tables. Code.`
2. (4 to 9s) Keep scrolling past math and a diagram. Caption: `Math. Diagrams.`
3. (9 to 13s) Export, then open the saved HTML file and scroll it. Caption: `All of it, in the file you send.`
4. (13 to 15s) End card.

**Post caption:**
Tables, code, math, diagrams, footnotes. The things AI output is full of, styled and carried into the export.

---

## Reel 5: The honest one (talking head or text, ~18s)

Trust content. Say the limits out loud. This is the one that makes people believe the rest.

**Voiceover or on-screen text:**
1. A few honest things about markdown.style.
2. It is free. No account, no limits.
3. Nothing you paste gets uploaded. It runs in your browser.
4. The PDF is Save as PDF from your browser. There is no magic export button, the browser just does it better.
5. It styles and exports. It will not replace your editor.
6. (end card) That is the whole pitch.

**Post caption:**
No account, no upload, no catch. Free and open source. Try it with your last AI answer.

---

## Posting notes

- Reel 1 is the one to boost or pin. It explains the product in fifteen silent seconds.
- Space them out. One every few days beats all five at once.
- The first frame is the thumbnail people scroll past, so start on the raw markdown or the finished document,
  never a logo.
- Reply to comments in the first hour. Same rule as everywhere else.
